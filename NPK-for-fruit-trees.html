NPK for Fruit Trees: A Grower’s Guide to Balanced Nutrition and Bigger Harvests

Published on October 20, 2025

If you’ve ever wondered why your fruit trees aren’t producing as much fruit as expected—or why the fruit lacks sweetness, size, or colour—the answer may lie in the nutrients you're feeding them. More specifically, the balance of NPK for fruit trees.

NPK stands for Nitrogen (N), Phosphorus (P), and Potassium (K)—the three core macronutrients every plant needs. For fruit trees, however, the right ratio of these nutrients isn’t just important—it’s essential. Feed too much nitrogen, and you’ll get lots of leafy growth but few flowers. Use too little potassium, and your fruit may be small, pale, or lacking flavour.

This is where a specialised fertiliser like CompleteGrow’s Advanced NPK 4‑18‑38 + Calcium & Magnesium comes in. Designed specifically for fruiting plants under Australian growing conditions, it delivers a high-potassium punch with balanced support for flowering and fruit development.

Here’s everything you need to know about choosing and using the right NPK for your fruit trees.

What Is NPK, and Why Does It Matter for Fruit Trees?

Let’s break it down:

N – Nitrogen
Supports leaf, stem, and shoot growth. It’s essential for photosynthesis, but too much nitrogen during the fruiting phase can suppress flowering and delay ripening.

P – Phosphorus
Boosts root growth, flower development, and early fruit set. It plays a key role in energy transfer within the plant.

K – Potassium
Regulates water movement, sugar production, and overall fruit quality. It’s critical for sweetness, colour, size, and firmness.

When your fertiliser delivers the right NPK for fruit trees, you’re not just growing a healthy plant—you’re growing better fruit.

The Best NPK Ratio for Fruit Trees: Low N, High P & K

Mature, fruiting trees don’t need excessive nitrogen. Instead, they thrive with a lower-N, higher-P and K ratio.

That’s exactly why CompleteGrow’s NPK 4-18-38 formula is so effective:

  • 4% Nitrogen – enough to maintain foliage without encouraging unnecessary shoot growth.
  • 18% Phosphorus – ideal for flower and root development.
  • 38% Potassium – maximises fruit development, taste, and shelf life.

This ratio supports the tree’s natural growth cycle—from early bloom to harvest—without overstimulating foliage or sacrificing fruit quality.

Bonus Nutrients: Calcium & Magnesium

In addition to the ideal NPK blend, CompleteGrow’s formula includes:

Calcium – strengthens cell walls in fruit, reducing cracking and improving firmness.

Magnesium – aids in chlorophyll production and nutrient uptake, keeping leaves green and energy production high.

These are critical secondary nutrients that are often deficient in Australian soils, especially in sandy or highly irrigated areas.

When and How to Use NPK Fertiliser for Fruit Trees

Correct timing and method are just as important as choosing the right fertiliser. Here’s how to make the most of a high-performing NPK formula:

  1. Seasonal Timing
    Late Winter/Early Spring: Begin applying just before flowering to support bud development.
    Spring to Early Summer: Feed regularly to enhance fruit set and early growth.
    Mid to Late Summer: Support ripening, sweetness, and fruit quality with continued potassium feeding.
    Early Autumn: A final light feed helps the tree recover and prepare for dormancy.
    Avoid heavy nitrogen use in late summer or autumn—it can promote new growth that’s vulnerable to frost or pests.
  2. Application Guidelines
    Always read and follow product instructions.
    For liquid fertilisers like CompleteGrow’s formula, dilute with water and apply around the drip line (not directly at the trunk).
    Apply to moist soil and water in thoroughly.
  3. Frequency
    Depending on the product strength and tree size, feeding every 2–4 weeks during the growing season is ideal. Consistent, moderate feeding is better than one heavy dose.

Benefits of Using the Right NPK for Fruit Trees

By feeding your trees with a fertiliser designed specifically for fruit production, you’ll notice:

  • ✅ Higher Fruit Set – fewer dropped flowers, more fruit retained

  • ✅ Better Fruit Colour and Shelf Life – stronger skins and improved texture
  • ✅ Healthier Trees – less prone to nutrient deficiency, pests, and drought stress

And perhaps most importantly: predictable, reliable yields year after year.
